982. Triples with Bitwise AND Equal To Zero

Given an array of integers A , find the number of triples of indices (i, j, k) such that:

  • 0 <= i < A.length
  • 0 <= j < A.length
  • 0 <= k < A.length
  • A[i] & A[j] & A[k] == 0 , where & represents the bitwise-AND operator.

Example 1: 

Input: [2,1,3]
Output: 12
Explanation: We could choose the following i, j, k triples:
(i=0, j=0, k=1) : 2 & 2 & 1
(i=0, j=1, k=0) : 2 & 1 & 2
(i=0, j=1, k=1) : 2 & 1 & 1
(i=0, j=1, k=2) : 2 & 1 & 3
(i=0, j=2, k=1) : 2 & 3 & 1
(i=1, j=0, k=0) : 1 & 2 & 2
(i=1, j=0, k=1) : 1 & 2 & 1
(i=1, j=0, k=2) : 1 & 2 & 3
(i=1, j=1, k=0) : 1 & 1 & 2
(i=1, j=2, k=0) : 1 & 3 & 2
(i=2, j=0, k=1) : 3 & 2 & 1
(i=2, j=1, k=0) : 3 & 1 & 2

Note:

  1. 1 <= A.length <= 1000
  2. 0 <= A[i] < 2^16
